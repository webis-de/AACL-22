One-sidedness in Texts

About this HIT:
  1. Objective
    • Given two texts A and B, determine which text is more one-sided than the other.

  2. Instructions
    • An argumentative text wants to convince you of something. Some texts are more one-sided than others.
    • We will present you two texts on a controversial topic side by side (A and B), each including four arguments.
    • An argument can be either "pro" or "con" regarding the controversial topic. For example:


    • We call a text one-sided if it includes more pro arguments than con arguments (or the other way around).
    • We call a text more one-sided than another if it has more pro arguments than the other text (or con arguments).
    • In Example 2, text A is more one-sided than text B because text A has more pro arguments than con arguments, while text B has equal number of pro arguments and con arguments.
    • In Example 3, neither text is more one-sided because both have the same number of con arguments.

Many forms of energy have potential environmental impacts. The focus should be on designing regulations that minimize or eliminate these impacts. Regulations can minimize environmental impact of tidal energy

Bays and estuaries are always naturally "flushed" or cleansed and replenished by tide-waters flowing in and out. To the extent that tidal energy impairs the natural flows of these tides, it impairs this natural "flushing" mechanism. This can alter and even destroy an ecosystem. Tidal energy can impair the natural "flushing" of water ecosystems.

Tidal energy can slow the movement of water in a bay or estuary, which reduces the amount of kinetic energy and causes the body of water to freeze-over more often or for longer periods of time. This has consequences for marine ecosystems. Tidal energy can lead to prolonged and undesirable winter icing.
